## Chief Complaint
55-year-old male presenting with severe epigastric pain radiating to the back, nausea, and vomiting x 12 hours.

## History of Present Illness
Patient was at a family dinner last evening and consumed a large fatty meal along with several alcoholic beverages. Approximately 4 hours later, he developed sudden-onset severe epigastric pain described as "boring through to my back," rated 10/10. Associated with multiple episodes of vomiting (non-bloody, bilious). Pain worsened in supine position, slightly improved with leaning forward. He presented to the ED where CT abdomen showed peripancreatic fat stranding and pancreatic edema consistent with acute pancreatitis. No necrosis or fluid collections.

PMH: Cholelithiasis (not yet treated surgically), hyperlipidemia, social alcohol use (2-3 drinks weekly). **Allergy:** Morphine (severe pruritus/urticaria).

## Examination
- General: In significant distress, guarding, diaphoretic
- Abdomen: severe epigastric tenderness, voluntary guarding, decreased bowel sounds, no rebound, no rigidity
- Vitals: HR 94, BP 142/90, SpO2 95%, Temp 100.4 F

## Assessment
1. **Acute pancreatitis** -- likely gallstone-related (known cholelithiasis) with possible alcohol contribution. Ranson criteria at admission: 2 (age >55, WBC >16K). Modified CT Severity Index: 2 (mild).
2. **Pain management challenge** -- Morphine allergy limits opioid choices.
3. **Cholelithiasis** -- needs surgical evaluation for cholecystectomy once acute episode resolves.

## Plan
- NPO, IV Normal Saline 1000 mL/hr x 2 hours, then 250 mL/hr (aggressive early fluid resuscitation).
- Pain: Hydromorphone 0.5 mg IV q4h PRN (safe in morphine allergy -- different opioid class).
- Pantoprazole 40 mg IV daily (gastroprotection).
- Lipase, CBC, BMP, hepatic panel q12h to trend.
- NPO until pain significantly improved, then advance diet cautiously.
- General surgery consult for cholecystectomy planning.
- MRCP if biliary obstruction suspected (monitor LFTs).
- Monitor for complications: necrosis, pseudocyst, organ failure.
