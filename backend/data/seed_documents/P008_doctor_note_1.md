## Chief Complaint
52-year-old male presenting with progressive redness, warmth, swelling, and pain of the left lower extremity over the past 3 days. Fever onset 24 hours ago.

## History of Present Illness
Patient noticed a small abrasion on his left shin after yard work 5 days ago. Over the following 3 days, the area around the wound became increasingly red, swollen, and painful. The erythema has spread circumferentially and proximally. Yesterday, he developed fever (100.8 F) and chills. He presented to the ED after the redness spread above his knee and he had difficulty bearing weight.

PMH: Obesity (BMI 34), peripheral venous insufficiency, previous episode of cellulitis right leg (2024). No known drug allergies.

## Examination
- Left lower extremity: diffuse erythema from mid-shin to just above knee, skin taut and shiny, warm to touch, significant tenderness on palpation
- Demarcation line drawn with marker at borders of erythema for serial monitoring
- No fluctuance, no crepitus, no purulent drainage from original wound
- Original abrasion: 2 cm, superficial, crusted, surrounded by erythema
- Left inguinal lymphadenopathy, tender
- Right leg: chronic stasis dermatitis, no acute changes

## Assessment
1. **Non-purulent cellulitis, left lower extremity** -- extensive involvement, failed outpatient management criteria (fever, rapid spread). No abscess on exam.
2. **Peripheral venous insufficiency** -- predisposing factor for recurrent cellulitis.
3. **Obesity** -- contributes to lymphedema and infection risk.

## Plan
- Vancomycin 1g IV q12h (covers MRSA given recurrent cellulitis history and risk factors).
- Blood cultures x2 drawn in ED.
- Leg elevation above heart level when in bed.
- Erythema border marked: monitor for spread or regression q8h nursing checks.
- Ibuprofen 400 mg PO q6h PRN for pain and inflammation.
- Duplex ultrasound left LE ordered to rule out DVT (given leg swelling).
- Wound care: daily dressing change to original abrasion site.
- Anticipate 48-72 hours IV antibiotics, then transition to oral if improving.
